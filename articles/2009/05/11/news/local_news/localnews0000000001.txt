                        <p class="content">WASHINGTON (NNPA) - President Obama has announced plans to include $1.250 billion in settlement funds in the 2010 budget to bring closure to the long-standing Black farmersí lawsuit against the U.S. Department of Agriculture, the White House has announced.<br/><br/>ìThis is an issue I worked on in the Senate, and Iím pleased that we are now able to close this chapter in the agencyís history and move on,î the President said in a release. ìMy hope is that the farmers and their families who were denied access to USDA loans and programs will be made whole and will have the chance to rebuild their lives and their businesses.î<br/><br/>The White House is describing the potential settlement as ìjust one part of a larger strategy at USDA to improve civil rights enforcement.î<br/><br/>ìI am very pleased that President Obama is taking swift action on this matter as it is something that will help us chart a new course at USDA, one on which all USDA customers and employees are treated equally and fairly,î says Agriculure Secretary Tom Vilsack said.<br/><br/>A White House statement describes the sins of the U. S. government.<br/><br/>ìFor decades in the last century, USDA had a spotted history of discriminating against black farmers in its loan programs and subsidy programs. In 1999, the USDA entered into a consent agreement with black farmers where the agency agreed to pay these farmers for past discrimination in lending and other USDA programs. While thousands of those claims have been adjudicated, thousands of other black farmers never had a chance for their claims to be considered on the merits because of problems with the notification and claims process that made it very difficult for many farmers to participate,î said a White House statement.</p>

<h2>Davenport police say they are keeping eyes on problem area</h2>
<span class="by">By Dustin Lemmon | <span class="published">Thursday, March 02, 2006</span>
<p><span class="leave_comment"><a href="#qccomments">(30) Comments </a></span> | 
<span class="rate"><a href="#rate">Rate this article</a></span> |
<span class="default"><a href="#" title="Article default font size." onclick="setActiveStyleSheet('default'); return false;">Default</a></span> |
<span class="large"><a href="#" title="Enlarge article font size." onclick="setActiveStyleSheet('large'); return false;">Large</a></span>
</p></span>

<div id="story_content">
<p>An incident in which a student&rsquo;s jaw was broken at a Davenport city bus stop may have been the result of ongoing racial tensions between some juveniles, police said Wednesday.</p>
<p>Anthony Matthew Hall-Clark, 16, of 2214 Farnam St., has been charged in Scott County District Court with felony willful injury causing serious injury. He was out of custody Wednesday and his next court date is March 30.<br/><br/>Police said there has been an ongoing series of incidents at the bus stop in the 1700 block of Main Street, a block south of Locust Street, and the incidents have escalated in their level of violence.<br/><br/>According to a Davenport police report, school officials told police a group of black students has been trying to make it a &ldquo;black-only&rdquo; bus stop and has been picking on the &ldquo;frailest&rdquo; white students.<br/><br/>The police report said officers do not know whether the incident that occurred at 2:50 p.m. Friday stemmed from the ongoing problems, but it noted that Hall-Clark is black and the victim is white.<br/><br/>The police affidavit alleges that the suspect went up to the victim and punched him in the right side of the face for no apparent reason. The suspect then ran away, but he was identified by an adult school official who witnessed the incident, according to the report.<br/><br/>The victim was taken to Genesis Medical Center-East Rusholme Street, Davenport, and was referred to an oral surgeon for treatment, the report states.<br/><br/>Tim Wernentin, the principal of Davenport Central High School, said there have been problems at the bus stop in recent weeks, but he had not heard that they were racially motivated. He said the incidents, which have been limited to the one bus stop, have involved non-students and students from different schools, but declined to say which schools.<br/><br/>Wernentin said school officials have been monitoring the bus stop daily and have disciplined the students involved with everything from suspensions to detention and also have contacted their parents.<br/><br/>&ldquo;This week, (the problems) have de-escalated and that&rsquo;s been because there have been police and school consequences,&rdquo; he added.<br/><br/>Sgt. Eric Court said police have been patrolling the area more around school dismissal time because of the problems. He said they monitor the bus stop when Central and J.B. Young Intermediate schools dismiss for the day. Court said the dismissal times are about 15 to 20 minutes apart and that about 25 to 35 youngsters gather at the bus stop.<br/><br/>Dustin Lemmon can be contacted at <br/><br/>(563) 383-2493 or <a href="mailto:dlemmon@qctimes.com">dlemmon@qctimes.com</a>.</p>

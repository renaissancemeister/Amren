                            <td><p><span class="headline-detail">Rally planned to oppose  day laborers (1/6)</span><br>
                                    <span class="subhead-detail"></span></p>
                                <span class="byline-detail">By Dawnya Pring</span><br>
                                <span class="update">(Updated: Thursday, January 6, 2005 9:22 AM PST)</span></td>
                          </tr>
                          <tr>
                            <td background="http://tbrnews.com/art/dot.gif"><img src="http://images.townnews.com/tbrnews.com/art/dot.gif" width="3" height="16"></td>
                          </tr>
                          <tr>
                            <td><p><span class="story-detail">A rally supporting Redondo Beach Police for arresting day laborers illegally soliciting work on public land is being planned.<br/><br/>&quot;We are coming to Redondo Beach in response to the judge&#039;s restriction on the Police Department and city officials,&quot; stated a member of the Save Our State organization that is planning the rally. &quot;The Police Department was responding to complaints by business owners being harassed by these day laborers. We need to show we support the Police Department in their efforts, and send a visible message to the government that we will not be ignored, we will not be overrun.&quot;<br/><br/>Save Our State is a grass-roots political group that does not feel the government is doing enough to curb illegal immigration, states their literature. This upcoming rally is in response to a recent lawsuit filed against Redondo Beach by the Mexican American Legal Defense and Educational Fund. MALDEF filed the suit for a group of laborers that had been arrested during an undercover sting operation by Redondo Beach Police.</span><br><br><table border="0" align="right" cellpadding="4" cellspacing="0">
  <tr>
    <td align="center"><!-- AdSys ad not found for redondo_beach_news:instory --></td>
  </tr>
</table>
<span class="story-detail">After logging numerous complaints from residents and business owners about men loitering, littering and urinating on their property while they wait to be selected for a day of work by contractors or private individuals, city officials decided something needed to be done about it, City Prosecutor Mike Webb said in a previous interview.<br/><br/>Soliciting work on public property, such as a sidewalk, violates a city municipal code and hiring someone on public property violates another municipal code.<br/><br/>City Attorney Jerry Goddard argues that this is a quality-of-life issue and that the day laborers who congregate in these areas have been known to walk in the streets and cause traffic problems, and therefore the actions are also a safety hazard.<br/><br/>&quot;This isn&#039;t political speech, which is the most free,&quot; Goddard said. &quot;This is commercial speech. We&#039;re just restricting the time, place and manner.&quot;<br/><br/>Some 37 day laborers were arrested when undercover Redondo Beach officers approached the day laborers, pretending to hire them for the day, over the course of two days. Once the worker was in the undercover car and driven to another location, the laborer was arrested, according to Capt. Joe Leonardi.<br/><br/>The immigration status of the day laborers was not questioned and immigration officials were not involved in the operation, according to city officials.<br/><br/>The Redondo Beach Civic Center rally is also meant to counter a protest staged by MALDEF about a month ago when it announced its lawsuit against the city, organizers said.</span>

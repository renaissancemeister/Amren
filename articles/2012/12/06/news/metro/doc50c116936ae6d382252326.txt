	placeSbarTop = function(){

<p>
HAMDEN -- A redistricting plan will go into effect next school year to counteract racial imbalance at Church Street School, sending scores of students to nearby Helen Street School.
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
The move is necessary to comply with state law.
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
According to Superintendent of Schools Fran Rabinowitz, Hamden was asked to address the issue by the state Department of Education because minority students at Church Street School represented 25 percent more of the student body than is the average for other schools in the district.
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
To comply with state law, the new redistricting plan will send approximately 40 students from Church Street School to Helen Street School for the 2013-14 school year.

<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
The plan was approved Wednesday by the state Board of Education.
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
"The administration has been working on it since June, to come up with a plan that would have the least amount of impact on students and their families," Rabinowitz said. "We realize that it's very difficult to move from one school to another, but we have to comply with state statutes, so this seemed to be the very best solution."
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
Mayor Scott Jackson said undoubtedly this is a "difficult issue for parents because they come to love their local school."
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
"However, we have fine principals and fine teachers and fine support staff at both of the schools. So, I have every confidence that the children impacted will continue to receive a superior education. I personally went through a school closing as a student, so I know the difficulty. But I also know that the families will get through."
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
Rabinowitz said Helen Street School was chosen to receive Church Street School's students because it has the space available and it's close to Church Street School. Rabinowitz said the students chosen for the change won'thave to travel far to get to class in the morning.
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
"They're all within walking distance to Helen Street School. As the matter of fact, there are some students at Helen Street School that live farther away than these students do," Rabinowitz said. "Helen Street had the room and the same support that Church Street has, so we thought it was a logical choice."
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
An informational meeting is planned for January. Families involved in the move will be given the opportunity to learn more about Helen Street School.
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
"We are truly committed to making this move as comfortable as possible for the families," Rabinowitz said. "We intend to have them tour the school. We intend to have them meet with the principal. We intend to bring the children over to the school, have them participate in some type of an event at the school so that they will be very familiar with the school before the actual move takes place."
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
Because the redistricting is in response a state statute, Rabinowitz said she wanted to wait until the state gave its decision on the redistricting plan before revealing it to the public.
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
"I think that many of the parents may wonder why we didn't notify them in advance," Rabinowitz said. "We felt strongly that we were not going to notify them in advance until we knew if that plan was approved by the state Board of Education because, had it not been approved, we may have had to go back to the drawing board. And it didn't seem fair or right to alarm parents when in fact it may not happen."
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
According to a press release issued by the school superintendent's office, "This plan was presented to the state Board of Education in the context of a long-term plan for Hamden Public Schools." The town will be exploring possible renovations of Hamden schools to "accommodate growing student population and changing demographics." Also under consideration is the possibility that Alice Peck School may be reopened in the future, and the high school alternative program and central office might be moved.
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
Additionally, school officials are exploring via a feasibility study whether West Woods and Shepard Glen schools need to be renovated or if it is prudent to build new schools, or one new school.
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
Jackson said such a feasibility study is necessary to help chart the future landscape of Hamden's education system.
<p>
<!-- end articleparagraph1.pbo --><!-- start articleparagraph1.pbo -->
<p>
"It's the responsible thing to do," Jackson said. "We need to lengthen the time horizons of our infrastructure planning if we're going to save taxpayer dollars throughout the course of it. I am broadly in support of such a visionary review."
<p>

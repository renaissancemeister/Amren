Always Blaming Racism (II)

Stephen Sharkansky, Oh, That Liberal Media Blog, May 18

The Seattle Times reports today on the Seattle City Council’s decision to stop impounding the vehicles of those who are caught driving with suspended licenses. “Seattle close to junking its controversial impound law” . The lead:

After five stormy years, Seattle’s racially-charged vehicle-impound law is headed for the scrap heap.
Six out of nine City Council members expressed their desire to dump the impound law, which disproportionately affects African Americans, in a key committee vote yesterday.

There has never been any evidence that the law is inherently racist, or that the police are even enforcing the law selectively. In fact, the police indicate otherwise:

impounding itself appears to be color-blind. The Seattle Police Department reports that 90 percent of the drivers stopped and found to have suspended licenses have their cars impounded, regardless of the driver’s race.

Nevertheless, the fact that the law leads to a disparate outcome is enough for those on the City Council who are indifferent to public safety to insinuate racism and for the Seattle Times to mindlessly trumpet those insinuations.

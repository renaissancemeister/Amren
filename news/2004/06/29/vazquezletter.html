Vasquez Sends Letter To Mexican Leader

Idaho Statesman, Jun. 26

Canyon County Commissioner Robert Vasquez Friday issued a challenge to the president of Mexico, repeating his request for Mexico to reimburse Idaho for $2 million in costs incurred by undocumented immigrants and asking for Vincente Fox to arrange deportation of a Canyon County inmate from Mexico who has tuberculosis.

Vasquez’s announcement of a letter to Fox was the latest move in his campaign against undocumented residents, who he says “drain away tax dollars, lower the wages of the hard-working Americans, bring disease and crime, and create a threat to our national security.” (“Canyon official bills Mexico for $2 million,” April 23)

The letter to Fox was sparked by a speech the Mexican president gave recently in Chicago, in which he reportedly “pledged to help resolve any issues Mexicans living in the United States might have,” Vasquez said in a news release.

Previous statements from Vasquez prompted formation last month of Action Against Hate, a political action committee to combat what members characterize as distortions and hate messages regarding undocumented immigrants.

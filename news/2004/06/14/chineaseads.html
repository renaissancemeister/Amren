NDP To Launch National Chinese Language Ads

CTV.ca, Jun. 13

The NDP is taking advantage of their leader’s connection with the Chinese community. The party is launching a new series of TV ads featuring Jack Layton and his wife, NDP candidate Olivia Chow, speaking Cantonese and Mandarin.

Chow, who immigrated to Canada from Hong Kong when she was 13, taught Layton to speak Cantonese. The pair have a high profile in Toronto’s Chinese community and both have been active in Toronto city politics.

The ads will begin airing across Canada on Monday.

The commercials say the NDP will improve health care, reduction the cost of education and get rid of the GST on family essentials.

Another message in the ads may be of significant interest to Chinese immigrants — the party says it will speed up the recognition of international credentials.

On Thursday, Layton said he’d certify professional immigrants — such as doctors — before they arrive in Canada.

He also said he’d overhaul Canada’s immigration policy so that immigrants could start living productive lives sooner.

This is the first time the party has produced Chinese language ads.

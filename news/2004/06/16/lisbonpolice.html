Lisbon Police Chief Ordered Out For Racial Comment

Reuters, Jun. 11

LISBON (Reuters) — Lisbon’s police chief has been told to resign for saying black people are troublemakers, a city spokesman said on Friday.

Mayor Pedro Santana Lopes ordered police chief Jose Francisco Almeida Rodrigues to resign for comments about a cultural festival in an area with many African immigrants and black Portuguese, the spokesman said.

The mayor’s office contacted Almeida Rodrigues “about midnight last night and told him to hand in his resignation”, he said.

Almeida Rodrigues said in a letter recommending a festival permit be denied that the area was frequented “mostly by those of black skin, drug addicts and people carrying out prostitution”, Lusa news agency quoted the document as saying.

“Such individuals bring with them and carry out practices and customs of their origin, which would bring more disorder in those areas of the city,” the letter said.

The festival has not been scheduled.

Almeida Rodrigues defended his decision in an interview late on Thursday with private TSF radio. He said his reference to race was irrelevant.

“If we continue only with African music, it’s going to draw more Africans to that area and it’s going to bring problems fast, in principle,” he said.

Santana Lopes said in a statement the comments showed “lack of respect for untouchable values and principles”.

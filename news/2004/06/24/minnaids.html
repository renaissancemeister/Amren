HIV Cases Increase Among African-Born Minnesotans

State sponsors effort to curb the spread

Rachel E. Stassen-Berger, Pioneer Press, TwinCities.com, Jun. 23

African-born Minnesotans made up less than 1 percent of the state’s population but more than 20 percent of the new HIV cases last year.

That’s a five-fold increase in new diagnoses since 1996.

Those statistics and the illnesses they foretell are behind a new state-sponsored effort to prevent HIV infection among African-born Minnesotans. The state Health Department announced Tuesday that it would distribute $100,000 among 15 organizations to educate people from Ethiopia, Somalia, Liberia, Sudan, Cameroon, Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda and Nigeria about the disease and its prevention.

The grant organizations are: African and American Friendship Association for Corporation & Development Inc.; African Assistance Program; Ethiopians in Minnesota Inc.; Mestawet Ethiopian Newspaper; Minnesota African Women Association; New American Community Services; Oromo Community of Minnesota; Project Valentine; Somali Community Resettlement Services Inc.; Somali Health Project; Somali Mai Community; Sub-Saharan African Youth and Family Services in Minnesota; the Bali Cultural Association/Zyombi Project; Victory Chapel/Liberian Human Rights & Refugee Welfare Organization; and Women Light Action Network.

The increase in HIV cases among the African-born comes amid a decrease in total HIV cases in Minnesota. According to an April report from the Health Department, 266 new cases of HIV were reported in Minnesota last year, a 13 percent decrease from 2002.

White, non-Hispanic people made up more than half of all the new HIV cases.

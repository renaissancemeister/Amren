Cannon Bests Throckmorton In Bid For Re-Election To House

Nicole Warburton, Salt Lake Tribune, Jun. 23

From the quiet of his Washington, D.C. office, Utah Rep. Chris Cannon celebrated his victory Tuesday night over challenger Matt Throckmorton in the 3rd Congressional District Republican primary.

Cannon won a bit more than 58 percent of the vote to nearly 42 percent for Throckmorton with all precincts counted.

“I really wish I were at home with my family and supporters, but it’s important I’m here,” Cannon said in a telephone interview. “But, I’m relieved and I think this shows that the people of Utah are really optimistic about the future of Utah. I think I’ve done a bunch of things for Utah that are really important.”

Throckmorton conceded as the returns rolled in giving Cannon a large margin of victory. Still, the former state legislator described his campaign as a victory, pointing out he thrust a four-term congressman into a primary and had a strong showing in Utah County despite being outspent $307,510 to $37,006.

“In political terms, it’s a victory,” said Throckmorton. “In terms of public policy, it’s a stunning defeat.”He was referring to Cannon’s stand on immigration; Cannon wants to grant certain groups of foreign agricultural workers a temporary “guest worker” status, but Throckmorton and other groups have called the proposal an “amnesty” and hammered the congressman on the issue.

“Immigration reform” groups spent nearly $80,000 on radio ads and billboards to educate voters about the Cannon-sponsored bill they say would grant “amnesty” to illegal aliens, according to Cannon spokesman Joe Hunter.

He said the race turned nasty in the weeks leading up to the election, with supposed threats by “immigration reform” group ProjectUSA. Last week, ProjectUSA Director Craig Nelsen hinted the group might challenge the votes of some foreign-born residents, but the group never acted.

Nelsen said that Cannon was soliciting votes from illegal immigrants with aide Marco Diaz on a Spanish-language radio station.

Nelsen said the results of the election were discouraging, but he said his group would be “twice as busy” in Utah to educate voters about the need to strengthen America’s borders.

“It’s well known how difficult it is to overcome the power of corporate lobbyist money,” Nelsen said. “But until Congressman Cannon comes clean with Utahns and admits he supports amnesty for illegal aliens, we must continue to try.”

Rosemary Jenks, spokeswoman for NumbersUSA, another immigration reform group, said: “What the people are being told is not the same thing he’s doing in Washington.”

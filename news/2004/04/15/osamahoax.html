Girl Recants ‘Osama’ Part of Attack Story, District Says

Lona O’Connor, Palm Beach Post, Apr. 15

A 12-year-old Boynton Beach student has recanted a key element of her story about four boys attacking her at school because of her religion, according to a spokesman for the Palm Beach County School District.

But a spokesman for the girl’s family says she is sticking by her story that she was targeted because of her distinctive Muslim head scarf.

The Council on American-Islamic Relations alleged Monday that four boys at Congress Middle School attacked the girl, a seventh-grader of Pakistani heritage, because she wore a hijab, or head scarf.

A council news release said the boys pulled on her scarf and hair, hit her with a belt in the face and called her “Osama.” The incident took place on April 5, and a relative asked the council for assistance, according to council spokesman Altaf Ali.

Ali said he spoke to the girl and her family on Wednesday and the girl still says the attack focused on her religion.

She also told him that she had been confronted similarly before, and that students had called her “Osama” on several occasions. She said she told a sixth-grade assistant principal, who minimized the significance of the incidents.

But when school board investigators questioned the girl this week, she said nobody called her “Osama,” a reference to Al-Qaeda leader Osama bin Laden, considered responsible for the Sept. 11, 2001, terrorist attacks.

“It appears that somebody embellished the story,” said Palm Beach County School District spokesman Nat Harrington. “This greatly concerns us. We want to get at the facts.

“Based on what she told the investigators, this may not have anything to do with her ethnicity or religion. When children have a conflict, it’s not necessarily because of nationality.”

On Tuesday, the girl’s parents made a formal report to the police officer on campus, Ali said.

A spokeswoman for the Boynton Beach Police Department said she was unaware of any such report. The school does not have a full-time officer, but a Boynton police officer works part time there, teaching a course called “Aggression, Victims and Bystanders,” she said.

The Council on American-Islamic Relations suggested that law enforcement officials treat the incident as a hate crime.

A Racial Double Standard

Dave’s Diatribe — Unfair and Unbalanced

David Mullenax, Augusta Free Press, Apr. 23

I confess that I’m a news junkie. Each and every morning, I browse numerous local, state and national newspapers. Now, I don’t read them all, but I peruse the topics and headlines that are appealing or attention-grabbing.

You’d be surprised by how many remarkable and controversial stories you never hear about. Yet earlier this month, a story appeared in every local media outlet in the Harrisonburg area, and no one could possibly have missed it. Personally, I heard it on the morning news, on the radio driving to work and read about it in the paper later that day.

The story, which garnered so much attention, was of a flyer distribution by a white-nationalist organization known as The National Alliance. Flyers, or handbills, as they are officially called, were placed on residents’ lawns with the image of a blonde, white woman and the words “Love Your Race.”

Also printed on the flyer were the organizations name and a Web address. That’s all there was to it. Nothing more, nothing less.

The idea of someone placing flyers in a person’s driveway is really not that exciting, when you think about. Our family receives handbills all the time — from lawn-mowing services, cleaning services, chimney sweeping, painting, etc. My all-time favorite was when we received one telling us we were going to burn in a pool of fire for all eternity, never to escape, if we didn’t change our ways and visit the local church. In reality, though, it wasn’t so bad. The distributors weighted the leaflet down with a rock painted with snail images, so we ended up receiving a free lawn ornament.

However, there was something else about the local media stories that drew my attention. It was a resident’s reaction to the innocent and harmless act of distributing flyers. While walking her dog, a local resident discovered the flyers and was “annoyed and disturbed.” In order to spare her neighbors from the same anxiety caused by the “offensive and racist” material, she proceeded to pick up as many flyers as she could.

This is the point when I start to ask myself questions. Why would a person, telling someone to take pride in their ethnic identity, be labeled racist? Why does the press celebrate ethnic-minority solidarity, but slander white solidarity? Are they not aware of the racial double standard they promote without an ounce of effort to conceal it?

Regardless of one’s race, I can’t think of a more honorable thing to do than to promote identity, community and empowerment among one’s own ethnic group. African-Americans, Hispanics, Jewish groups and now even those of an alternative sexual orientation are encouraged to celebrate what is unique about them, but the same lack of identity is denied — in terms of acceptability — to those that are white. Sounds like supremacism by minorities, if you ask me.

Recently, on the cover of the March 15, 2004, issue of Business Week, an article appeared entitled “Hispanic Nation: Is America ready?” In the lead story, Maria Velzaquez stated that she sends her children to Valle de Guadalupe, a small town in Mexico, at least once a year. “It’s important that they (her children) know where they’re from, that their connected to their roots,” she said. Really? Is there any difference between that and “Love Your Race”? Not at all, but the press heralds the Mexican community for their ethnic ties while slandering and degrading whites when they practice the same positive message.

Even the AARP commissioned a study by the Gallup organization to question Americans of different ethnicities on civil rights. When asked how they felt regarding whites being a minority in the year 2050, 25 percent of respondents said they were excited. What? Fire and brimstone would rain down from the heavens if a white American said publicly that they were excited that the African-American population was regressing in America. Americans of all ethnic backgrounds would demand their firing, make hate-crime accusations and gather in mob-like fashion necessitating penance.

As a test case, I would love to see the NAACP, Nation of Islam or the Anti-Defamation League distribute flyers in neighborhoods with an image of an African or Middle Eastern woman on it and the words “Love Your Race.” How would the press and, most importantly, citizens react? Want to bet that not a single person would be caught on television proclaiming how offended, annoyed and disturbed they were? I will also bet you that it wouldn’t make the local news, radio stations or newspapers. However, if it did, reassured residents would tell you how culturally enriching it was to receive such a flyer and how blessed by diversity everyone has become.

Yet if a white person says the same thing — as happened here recently — about his or her race, they are labeled a bigot, racist, xenophobe or the dreaded “N” word. That would be the word “Nazi,” and not the other one you can’t say in public without having Al Sharpton, Jessee Jackson or Abraham Foxman show up on your doorstep with more than a friendly flyer.

David Mullenax resides in Fishersville. His column, “Dave’s Diatribe — Unfair and Unbalanced,” appears every Friday in The Augusta Free Press.

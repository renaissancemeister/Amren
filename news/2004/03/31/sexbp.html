Agent Allegedly Let Women into U.S. in Exchange for Sex

Daniel Borunda, El Paso Times, Mar. 30

An El Paso border inspector was arrested for allegedly allowing undocumented women into the United States in exchange for sexual favors, the U.S. Department of Justice said Monday.

Federal agents arrested Customs and Border Protection Officer Francisco Macias on Friday as part of a multi-agency investigation.

Macias, 52, was stationed at the Ysleta port of entry, where he inspected pedestrians and vehicles, FBI spokesman Art Werge said.

It was at Macias’ post between October and the day he was arrested that Macias, a nearly six-year veteran, allegedly met the undocumented women, the FBI said.

So far, the case involves two Juárez women who crossed into the United States regularly, the FBI said.

“Certainly, this arrest is in no way reflective of the hard work of the majority of men and women who are highly proficient and committed to the mission of Customs and Border Protection, which is protecting the homeland,” said Leticia Zamarripa, bureau spokeswoman.

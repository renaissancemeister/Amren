Poll Shows Age Gap in G.O.P. on Immigration

Julia Preston, New York Times, December 6, 2011

With immigration now a front-burner issue in the Republican presidential contest, a new poll shows a substantial age gap among Republican voters over whether there should be a path to citizenship for immigrants who are in the country illegally.

A majority — 57 percent — of Republicans who are 65 and older say that tighter border security and tough law enforcement should be the only focus of immigration policy, with no path to citizenship for illegal immigrants, according to a poll released Tuesday by the Pew Research Center for the People and the Press, a nonpartisan group in Washington. Only about one-quarter of this group, or 24 percent, favor combining strict enforcement with a path to citizenship for illegal immigrants.

Among Republicans who are younger than 30, the poll found, 42 percent favor a combined approach of tough enforcement against illegal immigration with a path to citizenship, while 30 percent wanted only enforcement. Among these younger Republicans, another 26 percent said that opening a path to citizenship should be the immigration priority, with or without tougher enforcement.

Needless to say, my views changed as I began living in the city. I was constantly exposed to Africans and mestizos and their barbaric culture. I was raised in a small village that was about 98 percent white. I was constantly surrounded by a culture descended from Germans, Irish, and English settlers. I had bible school (though I am not Christian any longer) in old churches decorated with stained glass and carved pews. I played in Queen Anne and Victorian houses, surrounded by the handiwork of my people. I would stroll through graveyards packed with the beautiful markers of those gone before me, the thick woods around the village untouched and pure. A town where I could gaze upon the night sky, watch clouds, listen to the sound of birds and trees rustling. A town with an old library where I could read whatever I desired in utter silence, save the occasional whisper, the smell of old covers and stained wood filling the air. I could walk the narrow back alleys in the darkest hour of the night and no harm would come to me. I had taken it all for granted and while I grew up in that village, I spoke against everything that had made it possible for me. Perhaps I deserved the fate of having to live about blacks and mestizos. I am glad I do though, for it has awakened me. Now I am in a liberal college, where we speak often of the suffering of minorities and white guilt is fiercely instilled. I have found that those closer to reality (those who have faced hard times, who understand what it takes to survive) share my sentiments, while those who live in a world of plush couldn’t disagree more. I don’t have to become shunned or join a leftist culture, I can lie, and I do. I will not allow myself to become polluted or lose my dignity while in college. I will flourish in college, and taking what I know, I will pursue my freedom. I will defeat them at their own game.

I will say this, the one thing that has always gotten in the way of me falling to pressure to join liberal culture, is my education in my European heritage. Knowing how rich one’s heritage is, how grand the civilizations were, has made it a treasure I will not give up. History saved me.

My blood was given to me by my ancestors, it is not my own, and it is my responsibility to protect it for the next generation.

Race, Evolution and Behavior now available in Russian.

J. Philippe Rushton’s classic work, Race, Evolution and Behavior (reviewed in AR here, can be purchased here) has been translated into Russian. Just released at the Moscow International Book Fair in September, it sells for 400 to 600 Rubles ($12 to $15), and is being snapped up by scientific libraries as well as the general public. Prof. Rushton wrote a special foreword for this edition, which includes essays by two Russian scholars. The translator, Dimitry Rumyantzev, has a PhD in biology and is also the translator of Richard Lynn’s Race Differences in Intelligence (reviewed in AR here, can be purchased here). The book is published by Prophit — Style, based in Moscow.

A different publisher is now working on translations of Race by John Baker (reviewed in AR here) and of Erectus Walks Amongst Us by Richard D. Fuerle (reviewed in AR here). Caleton S. Coon’s The Races of Europe is also scheduled for translation into Russian.

Russians are generally less brainwashed about race than Americans and Europeans, and these books are sure to find an excellent reception.

the matter of who might get the breakthrough “prizes”. His brief commissioned opinion on the plausibility of the existence of a Big Foot is a remarkable example of brilliance and courage and clarity that destroys at once any notion of sensationalism or any regard to “a show of hands” as a supreme test of validity. The looming question — ironically maybe more among Russian intellectuals than here in the USA — is why his work has been so entirely disregarded on American campuses. If, as one reasonably suspects, it has been shunned not for its dated aspects but for it surviving virtues — then what’s dated is not especially relevant? A layman gets the sense that science in this matter of human origins now has soul searching to do about deeming certain questions from the “bone” people as being mere “static” or “loose ends” when now it appears they were very merited and that in the whole picture of “Eureka narcissism” abiding mysteries turn out now to have been just as important as breakthroughs. ??

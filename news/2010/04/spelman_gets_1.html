Laura Diamond, Atlanta Journal-Constitution, April 16, 2010

The grant from ExxonMobil will allow the college to offer scholarships to women interested in technology-related programs.

[from Wiki]
Oread Institute was a women’s college founded in Massachusetts in 1849. It is thought to be the first institution of its kind exclusively for women in the country.

Two graduates of Oread eventually founded Spelman College, named after Oread graduate Laura Spelman Rockefeller. Laura Spelman became the (future) wife of John D. Rockefeller. One of her instructors had been the abolitionist John Brown, and her parents were longtime activists in the anti-slavery movement.

— — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — -

Spelman College, in Georgia, which holds the distinction of being America’s oldest historically black college for women.was founded in 1881 as the “Atlanta Baptist Female Seminary”. It was founded in the basement of Friendship Baptist Church in Atlanta, by two teachers from the Oread Institute in Massachusetts, with $100 given to them by a church congregation in Medford, Massachusetts.


In 1882, the two women returned to Massachusetts to bid for more money and were introduced to wealthy businessman John D. Rockefeller.

The American Baptist Home Mission Society (parent of the women’s society) made a down payment on a permanent site for the school in 1882, and early in 1883 the school moved to its new home. The balance due was paid by John D. Rockefeller, who in 1884 visited the school and settled the debt on the property. The name of the school was changed to the Spelman Seminary in honor of Laura Spelman, an Oread student and wife of John D. Rockefeller who helped to fund the school. Rockefeller also donated the funds for what is currently the oldest building on campus, Rockefeller Hall.

In 1924, Spelman Seminary became Spelman College. Spelman also solidified its affiliation with Morehouse College and Atlanta University .

In 1927, one of the most important buildings on campus, Sisters Chapel, was dedicated. The chapel was named for its primary benefactors, Laura Spelman Rockefeller and Lucy Maria Spelman.

Laura Spelman Rockefeller Memorial Building, completed in 1918, is named after Laura Spelman Rockefeller, John D. Rockefeller’s wife, who was a primary contributor to Spelman. It now houses the Marian Wright Edelman Child Development Center, and also provides a student residence hall. It is typically referred to as “Laura Spelman” to avoid confusion with the many other buildings named after Rockefeller’s relatives.

Abby Aldrich Rockefeller Hall (commonly called ‘Abby’ by students) was built in 1952 and serves as a freshman residence hall. The hall was named for Abby Aldrich Rockefeller, the wife of John D. Rockefeller, Jr.

John D. Rockefeller, Jr. Fine Arts Building was completed in 1964 and houses the Departments of Music and Drama.

Spelman has amassed an endowment fund of over $291 million, and was ranked at 77 in the 2009 U.S. News and World Report ranking of all U.S. liberal arts colleges. The 2009 U.S. News and World Report also ranked Spelman first among Historically Black Colleges and/or Universities.

U.S. Herpes Rates Remain High: CDC

JoAnne Allen, Reuters, March 9, 2010

About 16 percent of Americans between the ages of 14 and 49 are infected with genital herpes, making it one of the most common sexually transmitted diseases, U.S. health officials said on Tuesday.

Black women had the highest rate of infection at 48 percent and women were nearly twice likely as men to be infected, according to an analysis by the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention.

About 21 percent of women were infected with genital herpes, compared to only 11.5 percent of men, while 39 percent of blacks were infected compared to about 12 percent for whites, the CDC said.

20 — nooffensebut wrote at 3:17 AM on March 14:

I want to correct some statements that were made here about herpes infection. Most cases of genital herpes are caused by Herpes simplex virus 2. Herpes simplex virus 1 can also cause genital herpes. Lafferty et al in 2000 found that 21% of heterosexual women in the US with genital herpes have HSV-1. Both viruses come from the Herpesviridae family of enveloped DNA viruses. They are incurable because they hide in the nervous system like the chickenpox virus, which can reactivate as herpes zoster (shingles). Treatment for Herpes simplex can reduce transmission and symptoms. Serological testing for Herpes simplex is available, but the US Preventative Services Task Force recommends against its routine screening.

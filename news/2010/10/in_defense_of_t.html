 Novel is about the genocide of white people. 


Toledo, Ohio: Kyle Bristow — a second year law student at the University of Toledo College of Law, three-time elected Republican precinct delegate for the 29th precinct of Michigan’s 12th congressional district, and former chairman of the infamous right-wing chapter of the Young Americans for Freedom at Michigan State University (MSU-YAF) — has written a novel about the persecution that white people have been subjected to by non-whites and leftists throughout world history. The 270-page book is available for purchase from Amazon.com for the price of $17.76.

In the novel, which is entitled White Apocalypse, a rogue anthropologist teams up with a proponent of the Solutrean Hypothesis and a fiery lawyer in order to reveal to the world the shocking truth that carries immense cultural, political, and racial significance: 17,000 years ago, white people immigrated to North and South America from Europe, and when the Amerindians arrived from the Orient by crossing the Bering Strait roughly 12,000 years ago, the latter subsequently and systematically murdered the former. As the reader soon realizes, the powers that be will do everything that they can to prevent this controversial theory from being espoused by the trio, and during this action-packed, semi-fictional thriller, the epic adventure will take the advocates of historical revisionism from the forests of southeastern Michigan to a federal courtroom in Ohio, from the busy streets of Washington, D.C. to an Amerindian reservation in Virginia.

The author holds a degree in international relations from MSU, and while he was an undergraduate student there and as the chairman of MSU-YAF he hosted as speakers at public events numerous prominent conservative politicians and activists including: Nick Griffin of the British National Party; then-Congressman Tom Tancredo; Chris Simcox of the Minuteman Civil Defense Corps; Jennifer Gratz, who sued the University of Michigan for its affirmative action policies and took her case all the way to the U.S. Supreme Court; Mark Krikorian of the Center for Immigration Studies; and many, many others. These public events were protested by violent left-wing agitators and were covered by a myriad of national newspapers and television news shows. Bristow has been interviewed by Bill O’Reilly on The O’Reilly Factor and has been quoted by Sean Hannity on Hannity & Colmes. Due to the author’s steadfast advocacy for immigration control and opposition to race-based preferential treatment for non-whites, he has needed an armed police escort on no less than six occasions.

Kyle Bristow said, “I wrote this book to bring attention to the persecution that white folk have faced over the years. After doing extensive research, I have come to the conclusion that liberals and non-whites believe that the White race and its civilization — Western culture — should die. The actions and words of the Culture-distorters overwhelmingly confirm this belief.” Bristow also said, “I chose the title White Apocalypse, because there are two meanings for apocalypse: (1) the revelation of divine truth and (2) annihilation. Both definitions apply to the subject matter of my novel.”

To date, no less than a half dozen articles have been posted by leftist websites to condemn the novel — even though not a single sentence of the text had been released and reviewed by them. One left-wing detractor went so far as to accuse Bristow of using literature to “foment White Power” and called his novel “White Power propaganda.” A far-left website posted the response of Dr. Dennis Stanford, the Head of the Archeology Division and Director of the Paleo-Indian Program at the National Museum of Natural History, to an inquiry regarding White Apocalypse: Dr. Stanford condemned the fictional work — even though he had yet to read any of it — on the basis that there are “several major problems” with the pro-white themes.

To the New Zealander -

Waitstaff and bartenders in the United States are not paid even the official minimum wage. They are paid a small amount per hour (typically between $2 - $4) that essentially covers about 50-70% of their taxes. Most do not receive a paycheck, and must cover the shortfall in their tax obligation out-of-pocket. I worked as a waiter/bartender for years and made $2.38 per hour. The legal minimum wage at the time was just under $8/hour.

This is legal in the U.S. because it is accepted and EXPECTED that customers will tip. The industry standard for competent service is 15-20%. My establishment would add a 17% gratuity to a bill for parties of five or more to ensure that the waiter would be paid. When a customer does not tip, or tips a pitifully small amount, the waiter is not only not making any money - he or she is actually losing money. Taxes at the end of the fiscal year are compiled by the total gross sales of a waiter (not their reported earning in tips) as it is assumed the waiter is netting at least 10% of their sales in tip earnings. Waiters must ‘tip out’ at the end of each night, meaning they must pay their busboys and service bartender a small percentage of their earnings (again determined by their gross sales regardless of their actual tip income.) Clearly, when a patron then does not tip, the waiter is still responsible to the government (taxes) and fellow employees (tip out) for the assumed revenue.

This is why waiters are loathe to wait on blacks and, usually, Europeans. The blacks tend not to tip out of selfishness, while those from other countries (understandably) do not recognize that the waiter isn’t ‘a paid employee’.

Hope that clears it up.

Indian State Government Encourages People to Eat Rats

Melanie Lee, Reuters, August 18, 2008

{snip}

Authorities in Bihar, one of India’s poorest states, are asking rich and poor alike to switch to eating rats in a bid to reduce the dependence on rice. They even plan to offer rats on restaurant menus.

“Eating of rats will serve twin purposes — it will save grains from being eaten away by rats and will simultaneously increase our grain stock,” Vijay Prakash, an official from the state’s welfare department, told Reuters.

Officials say almost 50 percent of India’s food grains stocks are eaten away by rodents in fields or warehouses.

My point was that they’re all rodents, and that various people in various areas and times have eaten rodents.

Indians have not created this “eat a rat” campaign out of thin air. These rats are undergoing one of their cyclical population explosions - much the way other rodents do - due to several years of favorable climate and food conditions. We see the same thing in the US southwest with mice; several good years for grasshoppers also gets us a lot of mice in the four-corners area, and then hantavirus (a rodent-spread hemmorhagic fever) becomes an issue. In India, the main impact of this abundance of rats is the damage the animals are causing to the crops grown by peasant subsistance farmers. Other animals there prey on the rats, to be sure, but this predation does not really address the severe short-term crop damage. Besides: how many kraits and cobras does one really want around one’s village and fields, anyway?

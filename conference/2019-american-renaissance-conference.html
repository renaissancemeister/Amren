2019 American Renaissance Conference

A People Rising

The establishment is terrified. Giant media companies keep trying to silence racial dissidents even as they turn a bind eye to violent hatred against whites. Why? Because we threaten the very foundations of how they see the world. Our reach and appeal continue to grow — and they are afraid. We are right and our opponents are wrong. They have power; we have truth.

Come join the men and women who are leading the fight against censorship, calumny, and dispossession. Join us for a weekend of inspiring speeches, networking, and conviviality.

Speakers include:

Patrick Casey — “Woke Capital: An Unholy Alliance.” Patrick Casey is the executive director of Identity Evropa and a frequent contributor to Red Ice TV.
Sam Dickson  — “Under Arrest: You’ve Had Too Much to Think.” Mr. Dickson is an attorney who has been a advocate for our people since his college days. He has addressed every American Renaissance conference.
Nick Fuentes — “Revolution From the Middle.” Mr. Fuentes hosts a nightly YouTube series on politics called “America First.”
Faith Goldy — Mrs. Goldy is a Canadian journalist and political commentator who worked for the Toronto Sun, National Post, and Rebel Media before starting a weekly independent YouTube program, “Canada First.”
Lana Lokteff — “Living in a World Gone Mad.” Lana Lokteff is a wife, mother, and commentator. She is the host of 3Fourteen and co-host of Red Ice TV. Slandered by the legacy media as the “Queen Bee of White Supremacy,” she is simply passionate about the future of European peoples.
Ayla Stewart — “Your Nation Is Your Family.” Mrs. Stewart, known online as Wife With A Purpose, is a Christian wife and homeschooling mother of 6 children. She is also a writer, artist, and YouTuber who promotes traditional family values.
Jared Taylor  — “Why We are Winning.” Mr. Taylor is editor of American Renaissance. He is the author of Paved With Good Intentions and White Identity: Racial Consciousness in the 21st Century. His latest book is If We Do Nothing: Essays and reviews from 25 years of white advocacy.

Register now for the American Renaissance Conference

May 17 – 19, 2019, Montgomery Bell Inn and Conference Center

Conference schedule:

The conference will begin on Friday, May 17, 2019, with registration from 5:00 to 7:00 p.m. There will be a reception followed by opening remarks from the American Renaissance staff. Latecomer registration is at 8:30 a.m. on Saturday, May 18, and the program will begin promptly at 9:00 a.m.

There will be a banquet (separate charge of $35) on Saturday evening. Participants with alternate dinner plans are welcome after the meal to hear the after-dinner speaker. The program will resume on Sunday the 19th and end at 11:00 a.m.

Gentlemen will wear jackets and ties — equivalent dress for ladies — to all conference events.

We will prepare name tags in advance; when you register, you may indicate whether you would like to use a nom de guerre. Please contact us if you or your organization would like to set up a vendor table.

Accommodations and transportation:

The conference will be held in the lodge at the beautiful Montgomery Bell State Park. The address is 1000 Hotel Avenue, Burns, Tennessee. If you fly, it would be best to rent a car at the Nashville airport and drive the 45 minutes to the park. Rooms at the lodge each have two double beds and are available only through us for $99.75 per night. There is no extra charge for double occupancy.

Rooms at the lodge will almost certainly sell out. There are rooms available at nearby motels, but registering early will guarantee you a room on site.

Special accommodations:

There are a limited number of suites and villas available for those who wish to attend as groups.

Suites (1 king bed, 2 double beds): $267.75 per night. Villas (1 king, 2 queen, 1 queen sofa sleeper): $199.50 per night. Please call if you would like to reserve a suite or villa.

Payment:

Early-bird registration is $150 per person. After April 17, the price increases to $200 per person.

Please call us at 703-716-0900 to ask about student discounts.

Patron registration is available for an additional $200. Patrons will receive special name tag designations as well as preferred seating with conference speakers at the Saturday evening banquet.

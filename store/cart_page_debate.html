Racial Diversity: A Strength
or Weakness for North America

The Taylor-March Debate
Halifax, Nova Scotia
March 6, 2007

DVD: $14.95 (audio/video)
CD: $9.50 (audio only)

Jared Taylor, editor of American Renaissance, and Peter March, professor of philosophy at Saint Mary’s University, square off in the debate the people of Halifax were not allowed to see.

Mr. Taylor originally agreed to debate the pros and cons of racial diversity with Prof. David Divine of Dalhousie University in Halifax in January, but Prof. Divine got cold feet. When Mr. Taylor attempted to deliver a speech on the costs of racial diversity in Canada later that month, leftist thugs attempted to silence him by banging pots and pans before physically ejecting him from the room. Prof. March, no friend of race realism but a strong supporter of free speech, invited Mr. Taylor back to Halifax in March to debate the question on the campus of Saint Mary’s. Hours before the debate was to begin Saint Mary’s abruptly canceled, citing dubious security concerns. The university later allowed the debate to take place, under the following conditions: no audience, no press, and no advanced notice. Nevertheless, Mr. Taylor and Prof. March agreed, and a polite, civilized, and lively debate finally took place.

The debate was professionally recorded by Saint Mary’s University at an “undisclosed location” in Halifax on March 6. It is approximately 1 hour in length, and is available on DVD. Audio of the debate is available on CD.

“This so-called ‘debate’ is a provocation against the people of Canada and Nova Scotia. It is an attack on the rights of all. The opposition against this false ‘debate’ reflects the finest Canadian democratic tradition and declares that Halifax will not be a harbour for racism and fascism. We must ask why the Canadian state has allowed this ‘master race supremacist’ from the United States to re-enter the country.” — Prof. Isaac Saney, Dalhousie University
